## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Yes — this looks AI-generated.** The site uses every tell its own PRODUCT.md warns against:

- Numbered section markers (02/03/04/05) on every section — decorative, not informational
- Tiny uppercase eyebrow labels above every heading — "What We Build", "Why Us", "Our Work", "About Us", "Technology"
- Gradient text on hero ("NOW.")
- Identical card grids across services, portfolio, tech stack, locations
- Generic taglines ("Let's build something great.")

**Detector scan**: 2 gradient-text warnings found — `globals.css:91` (`.gradient-text-hero` class) and `Navbar.tsx:44` (brand logo "KR" gradient).

## Overall Impression

A site with genuine strengths (local-market authenticity, internal dark-theme consistency, solid accessibility) undermined by five key problems: a light-themed Footer that breaks the dark experience, AI-scaffolding patterns everywhere, zero error handling, animation overstimulation, and all contact paths forcing the user off-site.

## What's Working

1. **Local-market authenticity** — INR pricing, city names, WhatsApp-first contact, founder story rooted in a real observation
2. **Dark-theme discipline** — typography stack and color palette are consistently applied across all dark pages
3. **Accessibility foundation** — skip-link, aria labels, `prefers-reduced-motion`, focus-visible outlines on everything

## Priority Issues

- **[P0] Footer uses light theme on a dark site** — Footer.tsx bg-white vs rest of site bg-ink-space. Shatters coherence completely.
- **[P1] Site violates its own PRODUCT.md anti-references** — Uses numbered sections, eyebrow labels, gradient text, identical card grids. Agency selling design cannot use template patterns.
- **[P1] Zero error handling** — No 404 page, no error boundaries, no loading states. Portfolio "View project" is a dead affordance (motion.span with no href).
- **[P2] Animation overstimulation** — 5+ competing motion systems (blur reveals, count-ups, ticker, section-number slides, spring hovers). Violates "restrained accent" principle.
- **[P2] All contact actions require leaving the site** — No contact form, no inline fallback. WhatsApp/email/Calendly all external.
- **[P3] Section-number overlap risk on mobile** — `clamp(5rem, 15vw, 10rem)` numbers positioned absolutely may overlap content on small screens.

## Persona Red Flags

- **Jordan (First-timer)**: Jargon-heavy services (n8n, Supabase, Lighthouse), no "start here" guidance, must leave site to contact → high abandonment
- **Riley (Stress tester)**: No 404 page, no error boundaries, no empty states, no form validation → fragile
- **Casey (Mobile)**: Section numbers overlap risk, top-heavy nav not thumb-friendly → medium friction

## Minor Observations

- CountUp uses setInterval (50ms) — requestAnimationFrame would perform better on low-end devices
- Portfolio "View project" span on home page has no link — dead affordance
- No JSON-LD structured data (Organization, LocalBusiness) — SEO miss for local agency
- Font loading via @fontsource packages rather than next/font — misses Next.js optimization
- Ticker includes "CONTACT", "PURANPUR", "PILIBHIT" — not services, confusing in service ticker

## Questions to Consider

- Does the Footer light theme have a reason, or is it an artifact from an earlier iteration?
- Would a single, deliberate entrance animation (instead of 5 competing systems) feel more premium?
- What is the fallback path for a user who can't use WhatsApp on their device?
